Methodology Document (Manual Research Style)
1. City & Segment Selection
I chose Hyderabad because it is a major hub for biotech, pharma, specialty chemicals, and medical devices, with strong R&D presence in Genome Valley.

I focused on Basket A (Specialty Biotech, Diagnostics, Chemicals, Performance Chemicals) and Basket B (Medical Devices, Sensors, Animal Health) since these industries show clear differentiation, growth signals, and structured operations.

2. Research Process
I followed a manual research workflow to identify companies:

Initial Scoping

Used Google search with keywords like “biotech companies Hyderabad,” “specialty chemicals Genome Valley,” “medical devices Hyderabad”.

Cross-checked results with industry directories (e.g., Pharmabiz listings, CII Hyderabad members, Genome Valley company lists).

Company Verification

Visited each company’s official website to confirm they are producers (E1) with in-house facilities.

Checked location (E2) to ensure operational presence in Hyderabad (not just sales offices).

Evidence Collection

Looked for patents, certifications, R&D centres, ERP/SAP mentions, hiring activity on LinkedIn, and expansion news.

Verified leadership background (science-founder vs operator-founder) via LinkedIn profiles, press releases, and company bios.

Scoring (C3–C8)

Applied the Federer scoring rubric:

C3 Differentiation → patents, proprietary products, specialized equipment.

C4 Decision-Maker Quality → founder pedigree (PhD/IIT/IISc) or operator systems thinking.

C5 Growing Sector → tailwinds like PLI schemes, Make-in-India, China+1.

C6 Growth Signals → hiring, facility expansion, certifications, updated websites.

C7 Systems Maturity → ERP/SAP, dashboards, structured costing models.

C8 Leadership Succession → gen-2 involvement, professional managers, independent directors.

Fail List Documentation

Excluded companies that were service-only labs, PE-owned, subsidiaries of large groups, or generic pharma producers.

Example: Novopor Advanced Science (Bain Capital-owned) → fails Federer test due to institutional control.

3. Sources Used
Company websites (for products, facilities, certifications).

LinkedIn (for hiring signals, ERP mentions, leadership backgrounds).

News portals (Business Standard, Pharmabiz, Economic Times) for expansion announcements.

Government registries (MCA filings, DSIR recognition, USFDA/EU-GMP approvals).

Industry associations (CII Hyderabad, Pharmexcil, Genome Valley directories).

4. Learnings
Hyderabad has a dense cluster of biotech and pharma companies, many with DSIR-recognized R&D centres and global certifications.

Specialty producers (biotech, diagnostics, chemicals, MedTech) show clear Federer traits: growth mindset, structured systems, and promoter-driven leadership.

Roughly 30–40% of companies researched failed E1/E2 gates (service labs, traders, PE-owned), confirming the assignment’s expected yield.

5. Output
Final CSV includes 25 companies with detailed scoring and evidence.

Fail cases are documented separately with reasoning.

Personalization hooks highlight unique, recent achievements (e.g., Covaxin development, Genome Valley expansions, CE approvals).
